How to load DEMs

1. Go to http://www.opentopography.org/, and click on the ‘data’ tab

2. Zoom in on your area of interest and click ‘SELECT A REGION’, then click and drag to select the region to download.

3. Scroll down below the map and click ‘Get Data’

4.Set the Output Coordinate System to UTM (this is the only currently supported coordinate system).

5.. Scroll down to the ‘DEM Generation (Local Gridding)’ section of the page.

Here, set Grid Resolution appropriately. Directly below the map is a box with the text ‘The selection area contains approximately N points.’ An appropriate grid resolution is one such that N/Resolution2<1,000,000.

Also, set ‘Grid Format’ to GeoTiff.

6.. Scroll to the bottom of the page. Enter your email address in the email field (results will be sent to this address), and then click Submit.

7. Wait. Depending on the size of the region you selected and server load, it can take anywhere from a few seconds to a few minutes to complete.

8. Once processing completes, there will be a page section titled DEM Results. Scroll here and click on dems.tar.gz to download the DEM file.

9. Optional: Rename dems.tar.gz to something more descriptive.

10. Repeat steps 1-9 for any additional areas of interest.

11. Connect your Android device to your computer. Create a folder on the device called dem and then extract the contents of the .tar.gz files to this folder.

Note: If you don’t already have software for extracting .tar.gz files, you can download free software such as 7zip (Windows) or Zipeg (Mac or Windows).

12. Download the app from the Play Store

13. Open the app and it will now be able to use your DEMs.
